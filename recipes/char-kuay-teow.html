Char Kuay Teow

Description

Char kway teow (Chinese: 炒粿條) is a stir-fried rice noodle dish from Maritime Southeast Asia of southern Chinese origin. In Hokkien and Teochew, char means 'stir-fried' and kway teow refers to flat rice noodles.

It is made from flat rice noodles (Chinese: 河粉) or kway teow (Chinese: 粿條) of approximately 1 cm or about 0.5 cm in width, stir-fried over very high heat with garlic, light and dark soy sauce, chili paste, whole prawns, shelled blood cockles, chopped Chinese chives, slices of Chinese sausage, and bean sprouts.

An epic noodle dish from the streets of Singapore and Malaysia — big flavours, contrasting textures and the signature hint of charred smokiness, this is bold Southeast Asian food at its best.

Ingedients

  • 500 g / 1 lb fresh wide rice noodle
  • 2 tbsp lard
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 10 small prawns/shrimp
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 1 Chinese sausage / Lup Chong Sausage
  • 5 cm / 2″ piece of fried fish cake
  • 20 stems garlic chives
  • 2 1/2 cups bean sprouts
  • 2 eggs
  • 5 tsp dark soy sauce
  • 4 tsp light soy
  • 2 tsp oyster sauce
  • 4 tsp kecap manis / sweet soy sauce

Steps

  1. Heat 1 tbsp oil in a large non stick skillet over high heat.
  2. When heated, add shrimp and cook for 1 1/2 minutes until just cooked through, then remove into bowl
  3. Add Chinese sausage and fish cake, and cook for 1 minute until sausage is caramelised, then add to bowl.
  4. Add 1 tbsp oil then add egg and cook, pushing in the edges to make a thick omelette. Once set, chop it up roughly using a wooden spoon, then add to bowl.
  5. Add bean sprouts and cook for about 1 minute until just starting to wilt, then add to bowl.
  6. Add lard. Once melted and starting to smoke, add garlic then immediately add noodles. Fold gently 4 times using a spatula + wooden spoon just to disperse oil through noodles.
  7. Tip all the other ingredients back in plus the chives. Fold gently twice, then pour all the Sauce over.
  8. Gently toss 4 to 6 times to disperse the sauce, pausing in between to allow the noodles to have a chance to caramelise on the edges a bit.
  9. Remove from stove and serve immediately.
